# RiskPath - 90 Second Judge Demo Script

## Goal
Show meaningful AI usage, real-world utility, and working flow in 90 seconds.

## 0:00 - 0:10 (Opening)
Say:
"This is RiskPath, a prevention-first road safety assistant. It predicts risk before travel and reduces golden-hour delay by surfacing the right nearby services fast."

Action:
- Open app home screen.
- Point to Hero panel + AI metrics.

## 0:10 - 0:25 (Route Analysis)
Say:
"I will analyze a real commute route."

Action:
- In Route Risk Analyzer, click quick chip "Adyar to T Nagar".
- Click "Analyze risk".
- Mention India mode can switch to Global mode.

## 0:25 - 0:40 (AI Output + Map)
Say:
"The model splits the route into segments and scores each segment using weather, time context, traffic simulation, and hotspot proximity."

Action:
- Show colored segments on map.
- Show risk bar and AI Risk Engine Output JSON.
- Mention confidence value.

## 0:40 - 0:55 (Scenario Intelligence)
Say:
"Now I simulate difficult conditions to show dynamic risk adaptation."

Action:
- Toggle Rain and Heavy Traffic OR apply preset from AI Model Lab.
- Show risk increase and predictive alert.

## 0:55 - 1:05 (Safer Route)
Say:
"The system suggests a safer route and compares tradeoff between risk and ETA."

Action:
- Show route comparison table.
- Click switch route button.

## 1:05 - 1:20 (Emergency Layer)
Say:
"If risk escalates, user can instantly trigger SOS with location and emergency numbers."

Action:
- Click SOS floating button.
- Show 108/100/101 actions.
- Click "Copy emergency message".
- Show category filters for ambulance, towing, puncture repair, and showroom support.
- Mention live count of contacts fetched.

## 1:20 - 1:30 (Close)
Say:
"RiskPath combines AI prediction, map intelligence, and emergency support in one unified platform for real-world impact."

Action:
- Show export buttons (JSON/TXT report).
- End on dashboard panel.

## Backup Quick Line (if asked about AI)
"AI layer is our contextual risk engine producing risk class, risk percentage, confidence, and factor-wise reasoning from weather, night, traffic, and hotspot proximity."
